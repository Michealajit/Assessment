AICPA SOC Reports

Outsourcing in on the rise despite increasing cybersecurity breaches. In today’s challenging world of Blockchain, AI, IoT, and Cloud, you need to be a step ahead of your competitors. Think of the AICPA SOC report, also known as the SSAE 18 report as your company’s “Security Best Practices”. You need to demonstrate a level of confidence that your organization can handle your clients’ most confidential and valuable information, have the procedures and controls in place to provide the required assurance. SOC stands for System and Organization Controls. SSAE 18 SOC reports are often used for Vendor Risk Management and for SOX compliance. A SOC 2 Type 2 report or SOC 1 Type 2 report provides the much needed assurance of operative effectiveness of controls.

Data Security & Privacy are increasing concerns for many organizations. This is especially important in cases where data is regulated or sensitive as in case of compliance requirements for HIPAA, PCI, CCPA, EU-GDPR etc. Cloud environments are adding to the complexity of the issue. Privacy laws are being enforced that lead to heavy fines or penalties. SSAE 18 SOC 2 attest reports are commonly used for Cloud Data Security & Privacy controls uisng the CSA's Cloud Control Matrix(CCM).

Formerly known as SAS 70, later SSAE 16 and now SSAE 18, these AICPA SOC reports are being used for several years. The AICPA SOC Auditor is also known as Service Auditor. The SOC 1 mirrors the ISAE 3402 and SOC 2 mirrors ISAE 3000. The Service Auditor can issue a joint SOC and ISAE report. The User Auditor reviews the Service Auditors SSAE 18 report. SSAE stands for Statement on Standards for Attest Engagements. A SOC report is technically an Attest report and not an Audit report. An SSAE 18 checklist of Control Objectives can provide a guidance for SSAE 18 Compliance. The Trust Services Criteria 2017 is once such guidance.

A SOC 1 report is mainly used for ICFR(Internal Controls over Financial Reporting) in other words for Financial Data reporting. A SOC 2 report is mainly used for Non-Financial Data reporting. SOC 2 uses the Trust Services Criteria (TSC) as Control Ojectives or Points of Focus. The latest TSC 2017 is aligned with the COSO Risk Framework and covers 5 criteria namely Security(or Common Criteria), Availability, Confidentiality, Processing Integirty and Privacy. A SOC 2 Type 2 or SOC 1 Type 2 report provides if the controls operated effectively in an environment. A SOC 2 Type 1 or SOC 1 Type 1 report provides for controls implemented at a point in time or as on a specific date.
